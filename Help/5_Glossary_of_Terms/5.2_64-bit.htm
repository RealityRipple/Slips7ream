Section 5.2

64-Bit / x64 / amd64

The 64-Bit processor we use today was designed by AMD back in the early 2000s when the 32-Bit limit began looming. It successfully took over the market share of both Desktop and Server processors despite Intel's Itanium server processor's competition. Today, most people have 64-Bit desktops and laptops.
